I discovered heavy metal music as I turned 14, when my brother and I got <cite>[Rock Band](tab:https://www.harmonixmusic.com/games/rock-band)</cite> for the Xbox 360. He’d play drums and I’d play guitar. Those evenings rocking along to **Disturbed**, **Wolfmother**, **Judas Priest**, **Hollywood Undead** and **Coheed and Cambria** got me started.

At the time, I didn’t know anyone else into heavy metal, so I explored it on my own. I’d watch the Kerrang! and Scuzz music channels when I was at my grandparents’ house with Sky TV, discovering **Killswitch Engage** and _so much_ pop punk. I tracked my listening on [Last.fm](tab:https://www.last.fm/) and used their recommendations, finding **Atreyu**. I also looked at who was playing at Download Festival, learning about **A Day To Remember** and **Steel Panther**.

Now in my 30s, I’m no longer discovering a new band every week like I did as a teenager. But Spotify says my listening age is 19 – I guess I’m doing something right.

So here goes: this is how I find new music.

## Magazines

I’ve read every <cite>[Metal Hammer](tab:https://www.loudersound.com/metal-hammer)</cite> magazine since I was a teenager. The first copy I got was for a holiday to France, and I still listen to **Parkway Drive** and **Kvelertak** from that issue.

Before I went to Download 2015, Metal Hammer came with a CD of songs by new bands that were performing. That CD introduced me to **Creeper**, and I don’t think I’d ever have come across **Empire** without it.

My current routine with a new issue is to make a Spotify playlist as I read through it, adding a song for each interesting artist or album. As I listen to the playlist, if a song grabs my attention, I’ll save the album it’s on to listen to later. My recent favourites from doing this have been **CABAL**, **Castle Rat** and **Employed To Serve**.

Metal Hammer also does a <q>Top 50 Albums of the Year</q> list every December. I’m currently working my way through 2025’s.

## Support Acts

When attending a [gig](/gigs-ive-been-to/) for a band I like, I’ll always try to get there for the support acts. In the last couple of years, this has led me to **Lorna Shore**, **HEALTH**, **Calva Louise** and **Bilmuri**.

## Spotify

Spotify has loads of ways to find new tunes; this is how I use it: 

- For every artist, there’s a <q>Fans also like</q> section. This is where I discovered **Wind Walkers** and **PRESIDENT**.

- If I’m listening to a new genre, Spotify probably has a curated playlist for it. The last one I subscribed to was <cite>[misfits 2.0](tab:https://open.spotify.com/playlist/37i9dQZF1DXciq74vrQmM8?si=b402668af7e34f5a)</cite>, and it introduced me to **Cassyette** and **Scene Queen**.

- The <q>Release Radar</q> playlist does a good job of making sure I catch the latest tracks from artists I follow.

- I used to listen to my <q>Discover Weekly</q> playlist. I came across **Set To Stun** – one of my favourite bands – that way. However, at some point it ran out of steam and stopped giving me any songs I actually liked.

## How I Listen To Music

Now I’ve found all these tracks, albums and artists – how do I listen to them?

### Albums

I mostly listen to albums. If I like an album, I’ll save it on Spotify. If I love an album, I’ll buy the CD.

### Playlists

I have one massive playlist where I chuck in any song I like. If I just want music for music’s sake, I’ll stick this on shuffle – like it’s my own personal comfort radio station.

## Bonus: Asking People

If you find yourself chatting to someone about music, ask what they’re listening to, or if they’re going to any gigs soon. While sharing a room with a Canadian guy in a Buddhist temple in South Korea, he introduced me to **Heaven Shall Burn**.
