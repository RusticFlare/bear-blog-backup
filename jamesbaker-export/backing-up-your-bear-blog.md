I recently noticed in the [Bear Blog Changelog](tab:https://docs.bearblog.dev/changelog/) that you can export your posts and pages as Markdown files.

I used this new feature to create [a backup of my blog on GitHub](tab:https://github.com/RusticFlare/bear-blog-backup). You can also check out [a template](tab:https://github.com/RusticFlare/bear-blog-backup-template) I made so that you can back up your own blog.

## Why Though?

Firstly, I get the full versioned history of my blog. This is especially useful for the CSS!

Secondly, one of the key reasons I have my own website is to own the content. By exporting it:

- I’m able to move off Bear if I ever want to (hopefully not soon – I have a lifetime licence)
- I’m protected if (God forbid) Bear ever loses my posts
